Rika was born from a quiet rebellion against mass production — a belief that everyday objects should be cherished, not discarded.

Our story

Founded in a small seaside town, Rika began as a tiny workshop where we restored vintage kitchenware and carved wooden utensils. Over time, we collaborated with local potters, weavers, and ethical makers who shared our philosophy: slow, honest, and beautiful.

Today, we partner with artisan collectives across the globe — from linen weavers in Lithuania to ceramic studios in Portugal. Every piece is chosen for its durability, natural materials, and timeless feel. We're not just selling products; we're preserving skills that deserve to be celebrated.

Our name, Rika, reflects the two anchors of a meaningful home: the welcoming harbor that connects us to the world, and the hearth that gathers family and friends. We invite you to explore our collection and make these objects part of your daily rituals.
